7.        Superintendent – Dr. Byron Hurst. 

*            A school resource officer was hired for the high school. 

*            The Bogalusa City Schools District signed an MOU with Crime Stoppers.  If students or citizens witness a crime, they can report it on the Crime Stoppers app.  The information is collected at the sheriff's office and given to Phlesher Mingo, Supervisor of Child Welfare and Attendance.   

*            We will also implement panic buttons that students and employees can utilize if something should happen on the campus to alert authorities. 

*            We will order halos for the students' restroom and extra school cameras.  Halos are devices mounted on the ceiling that detect vaping, smoking in the restrooms, or any disturbance.

*            Training will be offered to the staff to discuss bullying to ensure they understand that it is adequately documented when a student reports bullying.

*            Our first district-wide professional development will discuss the Seven Mindsets Curriculum, a social and emotional learning curriculum.

*            We are rekeying all the doors to the central office.  Once all the schools have cameras, we will install cameras in the main office.  We have some employees who work after hours and want them to be safe.

           *            The high school will have one point of entry.  All visitors will have to enter through the front office.

           *            Landscaping will also be added to the high school.

*            The cafeteria at the high school has started serving fresh salads to the students, and they have been

popular.

*            The school administrators had Attendance Training provided by the Louisiana Department of Education.

*            The Bogalusa City Schools will use an online platform for board meetings called Simbli. This platform will hold all our board meeting agendas and minutes for public view.

            *           Taryn Spikes-Webb presented to the board the new website of the Bogalusa City Schools and also

introduced a new app to keep the parents informed.
