Bogalusa School Board

Bogalusa, Louisiana

Special Board Meeting

July 1, 2011

5:00P.M.

 

Proceedings Of The Bogalusa School Board Taken At A Special Board Meeting That Was Held On Friday, July 1, 2011

 

The Bogalusa School Board met in a special session at the Bogalusa School Board Office, in the Media Center, located at 1705 Sullivan Drive, Bogalusa, Louisiana on Friday, July 1, 2011, beginning at 5:00P.M. The sole purpose of the meeting was to select a Superintendent for Bogalusa City Schools.  The agenda included an Executive Session.

 

1.)        Call to Order

President Paul Kates called the meeting to order at 5:00P.M.

 

2.)        Pledge of Allegiance

President Kates led the Pledge of Allegiance.

 

3.)        Prayer

Reverend Raymond Mims delivered the opening prayer.

 

4.)        Roll Call

There were present:  President Mr. Paul Kates; Vice President Mr. Adam Kemp; Members:  Mr. Curtis Creel, Mrs. Eleanor Duke, Mr. Calvin Hymel, Reverend Raymond Mims and Dr. Brad Williams

 

There was absent:  None

 

5.)        New Business

 

A.)        Personnel

 

INTERIM SUPERINTENDENT’S RECOMMENDATIONS

 

Permission to Advertise

  1. Assistant Principal at Bogalusa High School

  2. 7 Elementary Teachers

  1. 2 replacing vacated positions at Byrd avenue

  2. 2 replacing vacated positions and 2 additional position at Superior Avenue

  3. 1 replacing vacated position at Bogalusa Middle School

  4. 1 replacing vacated part-time position

 

It was motioned by Mr. Hymel, seconded by Reverend Mims to accept the Personnel Agenda as recommended by the Interim Superintendent’s recommendations.  The motion was unanimously carried.

 

At this point President Kates asked for a motion to enter into an Executive Session.

 

It was motioned by Dr. Williams, seconded by Mr. Hymel to enter into an Executive Session.  The motion was carried unanimously.

 

B.)        Executive Session-Selection of Superintendent

 

Returning to the Media Center President Kates asked for a motion to resume to special session.

 

It was motioned by Reverend Mims, seconded by Mr. Hymel to resume to the special session.  The motion was carried unanimously.

 

At this point President Kates stated, ”At this present time we need a motion for whom this Board will accept as Superintendent.”

 

It was motioned by Dr. Williams, seconded by Mr. Hymel to accept Mrs. Louise Smith as Superintendent.  The motion was not carried unanimously, at this point.  The out come was a roll Call Vote as follow:

 

YEAS:  Dr. Brad Williams, Mr. Adam Kemp, Mr. Curtis Creel, Mrs. Eleanor Duke, and Mr. Calvin Hymel

NAYS:  Reverend Raymond Mims and Mr. Paul Kates

 

The motion was carried 5-2 in favor of Mrs. Louise Smith as Superintendent of Bogalusa City School District.

 

President Kates stated, ”At this time, we will say that Mrs. Louise Smith won the Superintendent Job.  I was in favor of Mr. Jackson because I felt that he was a better leader; but everybody has their own opinion about when they interview and who they judge.  So, I feel that we have Mrs. Louise Smith now so we have to work with her; and we have to start moving this system forward.  That’s the consensus of the Board.  Mrs. Louise Smith is our Superintendent,” concluded President Kates.

 

Statement from Dr. Williams-”We are just going to have to; the best we can, let the children know that we care about them; and know what the value of having a good education is.”

 

Statement from Mrs. Duke-”And I’d like to say that I have appreciated working with this School Board.  We have dealt with this for the last four weeks, I guess.  We have read everything; we have contacted; and I think a lot of research has gone into finding a Superintendent.  I think Mr. Jackson would have been as good as Mrs. Smith; I don’t know; but we shall see,” concluded Mrs. Duke.

 

At this point President Kates asked if there were any further comments.  There were none.  The meeting was adjourned.

 

6.)        Adjourn

There being no further comments or business to address, President Kates adjourned at 5:40P.M.
